# AI Evaluation Methodology

This document describes the structured methodology used throughout this portfolio to evaluate AI-generated responses and benchmark AI systems.

---

# Evaluation Process

## Step 1 — Understand the User's Intent

- Identify the user's primary objective.
- Distinguish explicit instructions from implied expectations.
- Note any constraints or success criteria.

---

## Step 2 — Identify Evaluation Criteria

Each response is evaluated using the most relevant criteria, including:

- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Clarity
- Reasoning
- Instruction Following
- Hallucination Detection
- Helpfulness
- Safety (when applicable)

---

## Step 3 — Evaluate the Response

Assess the response against each criterion independently.

Every score must be supported by evidence rather than personal preference.

---

## Step 4 — Record Evidence

For each criterion:

- Identify strengths.
- Identify weaknesses.
- Document supporting observations.
- Explain how the evidence influenced the assigned score.

---

## Step 5 — Assign Scores

Each criterion receives an individual score using the project's evaluation rubric.

Scores are based on documented observations rather than intuition.

---

## Step 6 — Make a Final Decision

Based on the overall evaluation:

- Accept
- Minor Revision
- Major Revision
- Reject

The final decision is supported by the combined evidence collected during evaluation.

---

## Step 7 — Recommend Improvements

Where applicable:

- Identify missing information.
- Recommend specific revisions.
- Explain how the response could better satisfy the user's request.

---

# Evaluation Principles

The methodology emphasizes:

- Evidence-based reasoning
- Consistent evaluation
- Transparent scoring
- Repeatable workflows
- Clear documentation
- Objective decision-making

This framework is applied consistently across all evaluation projects in this repository.
